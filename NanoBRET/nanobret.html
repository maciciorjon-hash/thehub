BRET ratio, mBU, Z′/Z factor & 4PL dose-response fitting for the Promega NanoBRET™ PPI assay (TM439)

Plate Data

Choose 96-well (8×12) or 384-well (16×24) format first — switching formats clears loaded data. Import a CSV for the donor (NanoLuc, 460nm) channel and a separate CSV for the acceptor (HaloTag-ligand, 618nm) channel; each should have plate-shaped rows (letters) × columns (numbers), matching the same layout PHERAstar/plate-reader exports use elsewhere in this suite.

The combined preview shows the raw ratio (acceptor ÷ donor) for every well. Click any well to manually enter or override its donor/acceptor values — useful for spot-fixing a handful of wells without re-importing a whole CSV.

Endpoint / QC conditions

Define one or more +Ligand / Treated conditions and one or more No-Ligand / Untreated background conditions. Each condition needs a well range using the same syntax as the rest of the suite: A1-A3 (row range), A1:B3 (rectangular block), or A1,B2,C3 (comma-separated list).

Pair each +Ligand condition with a background condition using the Background dropdown — this is what's subtracted to get corrected mBU.

Reading Z′ / Z

Z′/Z factor measures assay quality from the spread between a positive and negative condition:

Z′ = 1 − [3×SD(+Ligand) + 3×SD(No-Ligand)] / |Mean(+Ligand) − Mean(No-Ligand)|
  • ≥ 0.5 excellent separation, assay is reliable
  • 0 – 0.5 marginal — usable but noisy
  • < 0 the two populations overlap too much to trust

Dose-Response fitting

One shared No-Ligand baseline well range is subtracted from every dose point's mean mBU — only the compound concentration should vary between dose rows, all of which still contain ligand. Add at least 4 dose points (concentration + well range) and click Compute fit to run a 4-parameter logistic fit and extract IC50/EC50, plotted against log-concentration.

R² below ~0.7 (red badge) means the fit is unreliable — check for missing wells, a baseline that doesn't match the dose-series plate, or too few dose points spanning the inflection.

What NanoBRET measures

NanoBRET detects protein:protein interaction via bioluminescence resonance energy transfer (BRET) between a NanoLuc® donor fusion (emits ~460nm) and a HaloTag® acceptor fusion labeled with a fluorescent ligand (emits ~618nm). When the two fusion partners interact, the donor's energy transfers to the acceptor, raising the 618nm:460nm signal ratio. mBU (milliBRET Units) is simply that raw ratio × 1000, giving a more convenient scale to work with.
