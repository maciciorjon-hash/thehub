🧫 22Rv1
Prostate carcinoma · ATCC CRL-2505
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · prostate; androgen-independent xenograft derivative (CWR22 lineage)
MorphologyEpithelial, adherent
Complete mediumRPMI-1640 (ATCC 30-2001) + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~37–40 h
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:3 to 1:6 · 2×/week
Key featuresAR-FL + AR-V7 splice variant (ligand-independent), TMPRSS2-ERG neg, p53 WT
Tip: The key castration-resistant prostate cancer model and the go-to line for showing that a PROTAC degrades both full-length AR and the antiandrogen-resistant AR-V7 splice variant (enzalutamide/apalutamide are inactive on V7). Much better behaved than LNCaP at the bench — attaches properly and tolerates trypsin — but cells do like to clump, so a short, gentle trituration before plating gives more uniform seeding. Great companion to LNCaP + VCaP in a 3-line AR-degrader panel.
🧫 A549
Lung carcinoma · ATCC CCL-185
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · lung; non-small cell lung adenocarcinoma (alveolar type II)
MorphologyEpithelial, adherent
Complete mediumF-12K (ATCC 30-2004) + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~22 h (fast)
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:3 to 1:8 · 2×/week
Key featuresKRASG12S, KEAP1 mutant (NRF2-high), CDKN2A deleted, p53 WT
Tip: Tough, fast, and tolerant — A549 is a good "sanity check" NSCLC line for CETSA, NanoBRET and cytotoxicity workflows. The KEAP1 loss / NRF2 activation phenotype is clinically and mechanistically important: expect high basal antioxidant capacity, which blunts oxidative-stress readouts and can sideline some Michael-acceptor warheads. Note on medium: ATCC ships A549 in F-12K; many labs maintain it in DMEM or DMEM/F-12 without issue, but stay consistent within a study.
🧫 HCT116
Colorectal carcinoma · ATCC CCL-247
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · colon, adult male; colorectal carcinoma
MorphologyEpithelial, adherent
Complete mediumMcCoy's 5a Modified (ATCC 30-2007) + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~17–21 h (fast)
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:3 to 1:8 · 2–3×/week
Key featuresKRASG13D, PIK3CAH1047R, MSI-high, p53 wild-type, CTNNB1 mutant
Tip: Fast, forgiving, and genetically well-characterised — a good "default" colon line for PROTAC / degrader viability & target engagement studies. Because it's p53 WT, it's a clean background to see apoptotic readouts after MDM2 or target-degrader treatment. The isogenic HCT116 p53−/− (Vogelstein) line is a useful paired control if you're dissecting p53-dependent effects.
🧫 HEK293
Human embryonic kidney · ATCC CRL-1573
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · kidney (embryonic); transformed with sheared Ad5 DNA
MorphologyEpithelial-like, adherent — but loosely attached
Complete mediumEMEM (ATCC 30-2003) + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~24–36 h
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:3 to 1:8 · 2×/week
StorageBelow −130 °C (LN2 vapour) — freeze medium: complete medium + 5% DMSO
Tip: Cells detach very easily — a firm tap on the flask is often enough and over-trypsinising kills them. For virus production / transient overexpression you almost certainly want HEK293T (SV40 Large-T variant, ATCC CRL-3216) rather than parental 293. Despite the name, recent lineage work suggests the line is actually of neuronal origin, not kidney epithelium — worth remembering when using it as a "generic" cell background for degrader work.
🧫 LNCaP (clone FGC)
Prostate carcinoma · ATCC CRL-1740
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · prostate, lymph node metastasis
MorphologyEpithelial, adherent — very loosely attached
Complete mediumRPMI-1640 (ATCC 30-2001) + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~28–60 h (slow, variable)
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA (brief) · split 1:3 to 1:6 · 1–2×/week
Key featuresART878A (androgen-responsive but promiscuous), PTEN-null, p53 WT
Tip: The workhorse AR+ line for AR-degrader / AR-PROTAC work. Handle gently — even medium changes can dislodge cells; pre-warm media, pipette slowly down the wall of the flask, and consider collagen- or poly-D-lysine-coated plates for assays needing even monolayers. The T878A mutation makes AR promiscuous (responds to progestins, oestrogens, hydroxyflutamide), so interpret agonist/antagonist SAR with caution. For androgen-free assays use phenol-red-free RPMI + charcoal-stripped FBS.
🧫 MCF7
Breast adenocarcinoma · ATCC HTB-22
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · mammary gland; pleural effusion (metastatic)
MorphologyEpithelial, adherent — forms domes at confluence
Complete mediumEMEM (ATCC 30-2003) + 10% FBS + 0.01 mg/mL human recombinant insulin
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~29–40 h
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:3 to 1:6 · 2×/week
Key featuresER+, PR+, HER2−; PIK3CAE545K, CDKN2A deleted, p53 WT
Tip: The canonical ER+ luminal-A breast model and the standard background for ERα / SERD / ER-PROTAC work (e.g. benchmarking vs fulvestrant). Remember the insulin supplement — ATCC's formulation includes 0.01 mg/mL insulin, and leaving it out slows growth. For hormone-independent assays, switch to phenol-red-free EMEM + charcoal-stripped FBS ≥48 h before treatment. Beware clonal drift — MCF7 stocks from different labs have been shown to differ functionally, so re-validate ER signalling if using an old vial.
🧫 MIA PaCa-2
Pancreatic carcinoma · ATCC CRL-1420
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · pancreas, adult male; ductal adenocarcinoma
MorphologyEpithelial, adherent — with persistent floating population
Complete mediumDMEM (ATCC 30-2002) + 10% FBS + 2.5% horse serum
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling time~40 h
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:3 to 1:8 · 2×/week
Key featuresKRASG12C, TP53R248W, CDKN2A deleted, SMAD4 WT
Tip: Don't skip the horse serum — ATCC formulates with FBS and 2.5% HS; dropping it changes the phenotype. The line carries KRAS G12C, so it's one of the standard cellular backgrounds for sotorasib/adagrasib benchmarking and for KRAS-targeting PROTAC work. A floating sub-population is normal and genuinely viable — if you want consistent seeding, collect the supernatant, spin gently, and pool with the trypsinised adherent fraction.
🧫 VCaP
Prostate carcinoma (vertebral metastasis) · ATCC CRL-2876
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · prostate adenocarcinoma; vertebral metastasis
MorphologyEpithelial-like; small tightly-packed clusters + floating clusters + debris
Complete mediumDMEM (ATCC 30-2002) + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 95% air / 5% CO2
Doubling timeVery slow — ≥2 weeks to ~50% confluence
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA (extended) · split 1:2 to 1:3 · every 1–2 weeks
Key featuresTMPRSS2-ERG fusion +, AR amplified, p53 mutant — reflects clinical mCRPC
Tip: VCaP is genuinely hard work. Expect up to 48 h to attach post-thaw and after each subculture, and ≥2 weeks to reach ~50% confluence with a dense mixture of adherent cells, floating clusters and moderate-to-heavy debris at all times — all of this is normal. Cells recover better in a T-25 than a T-75. Do not discard floating cells at medium changes or subcultures — spin them down gently and add them back to the adherent population. Cells attach as small tightly-formed clusters + some singles, then spread as flattened epithelial-like islands. Despite the pain, it's the line for TMPRSS2-ERG biology and a key AR-amplified mCRPC model for AR / ERG degrader work. (Handling notes paraphrased from the ATCC CRL-2876 product sheet.)
🧫 HAP-1
Human haploid · Horizon Discovery C631
Horizon page →
Organism / tissueHuman · near-haploid; derived from KBM7 (CML blast-crisis line)
MorphologyFibroblast-like, adherent (unusual for CML origin)
Complete mediumIMDM + 10% FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 5% CO2
Doubling time~24 h
Subculture0.25% trypsin-EDTA · split 1:4–1:8 · 3×/week; passage <20
Key featuresNear-haploid genome — single Cas9 cut = complete KO; gold standard for CRISPR screens and target validation
Tip: Haploidy means one cut = one KO — no biallelic editing needed. Watch for spontaneous diploidisation after ~20 passages; keep early frozen stocks and validate ploidy by flow cytometry for long experiments. Sensitive to over-trypsinisation (2–3 min RT). IMDM is essential — DMEM gives slower growth.
🧫 SU-DHL-5
Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma · ATCC CRL-2958
ATCC page →
Phase contrast · © ATCC
Organism / tissueHuman · lymph node; diffuse large cell B-cell lymphoma (GCB subtype), 17-year-old female
MorphologyLymphoblast-like, suspension (non-adherent), small round cells in loose clumps
Complete mediumRPMI-1640 (ATCC 30-2001) + 10% heat-inactivated FBS
Atmosphere37 °C · 5% CO2, humidified
Doubling time~40 h
SubcultureMaintain 0.1–0.8 × 106 viable cells/mL · split 1:2–1:5 every 2–3 days; feed 2–3×/week; post-thaw viability may be <30% — normal
Key featuresGCB-DLBCL model; CD19+, CD20+, CD10+; CDKN2A/B homozygous deletion; PTEN altered; del(6q13), +12; TP53 mutant
Tip: The go-to GCB-subtype DLBCL model for lymphoma target degrader work. Suspension growth means no trypsin — just count and dilute. Post-thaw viability routinely drops below 30%; this is documented by ATCC and does not indicate a bad vial. Let it recover at low density (0.5 × 106/mL in a 24-well plate) for 24–48 h before ramping up. Viability at saturation typically plateaus around 80–85%. Key genetic context: GCB phenotype (CD10+), homozygous CDKN2A/p16 loss, and PTEN mutation — relevant if studying CDK4/6, PI3K/AKT, or BCL2/BCL6 degraders in lymphoma.
