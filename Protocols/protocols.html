Simple Dilution (C1V1)

CalculatorCalculators
Dilutes a stock solution to a target concentration in a target volume, using the standard C₁V₁ = C₂V₂ relationship. Use this any time you need to prepare a working solution from a concentrated stock — compound dosing plates, antibody dilutions, buffer working stocks.
When to use it
  • Preparing a single working dilution from one stock (for a dilution series, use Serial Dilution instead — not yet migrated to this app).
  • Stock concentration (C₁) must always be greater than the target concentration (C₂).

NEB Gibson Assembly Protocol (E5510)
Overview — the 3-step workflow
Gibson Assembly joins linearised DNA fragments that share overlapping sequences at their ends. The HiFi Master Mix contains an exonuclease (chews back 5′ ends to expose overlaps), a polymerase (fills gaps), and a ligase (seals nicks). The workflow is: (1) PCR-amplify vector and insert with primers that introduce the overlapping sequences → (2) DpnI digest to remove template plasmid → (3) assemble the purified fragments.
1 — Design overlapping primers (before PCR)
  1. Overlaps of 15–30 bp are optimal for NEBHiFi. Design your primers so the 3′ end anneals to your template (binding region, Tm ~60°C) and the 5′ overhang carries the overlap sequence matching the adjacent fragment.
  2. Check that overlap regions do not form strong secondary structure and avoid overlaps that fall on repetitive sequence.
  3. The vector is typically amplified by inverse PCR (back-to-back primers) to linearise it and add overhangs. The insert is amplified from its template with forward/reverse primers carrying the vector-matching overhangs.
Overlaps longer than 80 bp can reduce Gibson efficiency. Keep them 20–30 bp unless you have a specific reason for longer arms.
2 — PCR → DpnI → cleanup (one continuous workflow)
  1. Amplify vector backbone and insert separately using a high-fidelity polymerase (see calculator).
  2. Without purifying, add DpnI directly to the PCR tube — it's active in Q5 Master Mix buffer, no buffer exchange needed. Incubate 37°C × 60 min.
  3. Purify with a PCR cleanup column. Elute in 15–20 uL. Measure by NanoDrop — needed for the assembly calculator.
  4. Optional gel check: run 2–3 uL on a 1% gel to confirm band size and absence of non-specific products.
3 — Gibson assembly reaction
  1. Set up on ice. Add DNA fragments and water first; add NEBHiFi 2× Master Mix last.
  2. pmols = (ng × 1000) / (bp × 650) — aim for 0.06 pmol vector, 2× molar excess of each insert.
  3. Incubate at 50°C: 15 min (2–3 fragments) or 60 min (4–6 fragments).
  4. Store on ice or at −20°C. Transform 2 uL into competent cells.
Tip
If you get very few colonies but positive controls work, the most common causes are: (1) incomplete DpnI digestion letting template through, (2) re-circularisation of uncut vector — confirm linearisation by gel before assembly, (3) poor overlap design. White/blue screening isn't applicable here; always verify by colony PCR and Sanger sequencing.

Western Blot

ReferenceCell Biology
What is this? Western blotting (immunoblotting) detects specific proteins in a cell lysate by separating them by molecular weight (SDS-PAGE), transferring to a membrane, and probing with a primary antibody followed by an HRP-conjugated secondary. The result is a band at the expected molecular weight, indicating protein expression level, size, and post-translational modification state.
Cell lysis
  1. Optional: pre-cool centrifuge at 4°C.
  2. Prepare lysis buffer on ice: RIPA (~50–100 uL/6-well; ~50 uL/12-well) + 1× PI (100× stock) + benzonase (1000×).
  3. Wash on ice: aspirate medium → cold PBS → aspirate PBS.
  4. Add lysis buffer, distribute carefully; incubate 10 min in fridge (redistribute once).
  5. Scratch down lysate (tilted plate); transfer to Eppendorf.
  6. Optional: centrifuge 4°C, 13,500 × g, 15 min; transfer supernatant.
  7. Keep on ice for BCA or freeze at −80°C.
BCA protein quantification
  1. BSA standards: 2, 1, 0.5, 0.25, 0.125 mg/mL in ddH₂O — 25 uL per well, in duplicate.
  2. Dilute lysates 1:6 (10 uL + 50 uL ddH₂O) or 1:10 for concentrated samples.
  3. Load 25 uL standard + sample in duplicate; add 200 uL BCA reagent (50:1 A:B); mix 600 rpm × 1 min; 37°C × 30 min.
  4. Remove bubbles with a needle; read at 562 nm; confirm R² ≥ 0.99.
  5. Use the BCA & Sample Prep calculator (not yet migrated to this app) to convert BCA concentrations into loading volumes.
SDS-PAGE
  1. Running buffer: 1× MOPS from 20× stock (1 L).
  2. NuPAGE 4–12% bis-tris pre-cast gel: remove white strip; remove comb after assembling; fill with running buffer; wells facing inwards.
  3. Load ladder (2.5 uL); run 180 V × 1h 15 min (20-well gel).
Transfer & blotting
Transfer buffer: 10% 10× Transfer Buffer + 10% EtOH + 80% ddH₂O (reusable)
TBS-T: 100 mL 10× TBS + 900 mL ddH₂O + 1 mL Tween 20
5% Milk-TBS-T: 2.5 g dried milk in 50 mL TBS-T
Sandwich: BLACK → Sponge → Filter paper ×3 → Gel → Membrane → Filter paper ×3 → Sponge → CLEAR
  1. Cut nitrocellulose membrane + Whatman filter paper to 7×9 cm; pre-wet sponges and filter paper.
  2. Assemble sandwich (black to black); run 90 V × 90 min in ice-cold tank.
  3. Ponceau × 1 min rocking; rinse with tap water; confirm even loading.
  4. Wash TBS-T × 5 min; block 5% milk TBS-T × 1 h RT; rinse TBS-T × 5 s.
  5. Primary antibody in 5% milk TBS-T overnight at 4°C; keep antibody for reuse (freeze).
Next day
  1. Wash 2× TBS-T × 5–10 min.
  2. Secondary: α-Ms/Rb (1:4000) + α-tubulin hFAB rhodamine (1:4000) in TBS-T; 1 h RT on rocker.
  3. Wash 3–5× TBS-T × 5–10 min; image.
Tip
For better signal-to-noise with phospho-antibodies, use BSA instead of milk for blocking and antibody incubation — milk contains casein (a phosphoprotein) which can interfere with phospho-specific antibodies.
