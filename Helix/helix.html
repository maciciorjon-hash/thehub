Genetic Code tab

Everything that converts between nucleotide and amino acid via the genetic code, in one place. Translation turns a nucleotide sequence into protein (choose reading frame and codon table). Reverse Translation goes the other way — paste a protein sequence to get back a deterministic DNA sequence (most common human codon per residue; not a real codon-optimization algorithm). The Genetic Code (Codon Table) below both is a pure reference — click any codon to see its amino acid, chemical class, and every synonymous codon highlighted, and toggle between DNA (T) and RNA (U) display.

Sequence Tools tab

Paste a nucleotide sequence and click Analyse to run Sequence Analysis (length, GC%, Tm, complement, reverse complement, MW) and ORF Finder (all six reading frames, colour-coded sequence map) together. As you type, nucleotides are colour-coded live (A/T/G/C) and a composition bar shows the ATGC breakdown with GC%.

Compare

Align two nucleotide sequences (FASTA accepted) to calculate percent identity, gaps, and mismatches. The alignment is displayed with colour-coded mismatches.

Vector Library

Searchable reference of common lab vectors. Filter by name, resistance marker, promoter, or application tag (e.g. "His", "CMV", "lentiviral"). Click a vector card to expand its map and details.
